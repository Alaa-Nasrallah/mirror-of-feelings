About the Play

The Mirror of Feelings is a live, interactive theatrical experience designed to help elementary school students recognize, understand, and manage their emotions in healthy and age‑appropriate ways.

Through storytelling, music, movement, and direct audience participation, children learn that all emotions are valid, and that they always have a choice in how they respond to what they feel.

The play follows two friends, Danny and Sam, as they prepare a birthday surprise that doesn’t go exactly as planned. What starts as excitement slowly turns into moments of anxiety, anger, sadness, and eventually joy.

As emotions become bigger and harder to manage, a magical character – The Mirror of Feelings – comes to life and helps the characters pause and reflect on what they are feeling.

Meet the Characters

Danny

Energetic, cheerful, and deeply caring. He wants everything to be perfect and feels pressure when things might go wrong. Through Danny, children learn how anxiety feels in the body and how calming strategies like breathing can help.

Sam

Curious, expressive, and sometimes impulsive. He experiences anger, sadness, and joy, learning that even heavy emotions can be released in healthy ways such as movement, hugging, or sharing with trusted adults.

The Mirror of Feelings

A magical, friendly guide who senses emotions and helps children understand them. Rather than naming feelings for the characters, the Mirror encourages self‑discovery, reinforcing the idea that everyone is responsible for identifying and expressing their own emotions.

The COLOR Method

A simple way to understand what you’re feeling and decide what to do next.

C – Check your body
Notice what is happening inside you
O – Observe your face
Your expression can give you clues
L – Label the feeling
Give your feeling a name
O – Open a choice
Think about what you can do with this feeling
R – Respond in a healthy way
Choose an action that helps you and others

What Is a Feeling?

Everyone has feelings. Feelings are signals from inside us, from our body, heart, and mind.

Some feelings feel light and comfortable. Others feel heavy, tight, or confusing.

All feelings are normal. They are not good or bad – they are messages from inside!

Feelings help us understand what we need, what matters to us, and how to take care of ourselves and others.

When we learn how to notice and understand our feelings, we gain a powerful skill for life.

Educational Value

Children experience strong emotions daily, especially in school environments – during exams, social interactions, mistakes, surprises, and changes. Without proper tools, emotions often turn into behavioral challenges.

Key Benefits for Students:

  • Develops emotional awareness and vocabulary
  • Encourages empathy and understanding of others
  • Teaches self‑regulation and calming strategies
  • Reduces impulsive emotional reactions
  • Strengthens communication skills
  • Reinforces trust in adults as emotional support figures

Take‑Home Booklet

The learning experience continues after the performance. Each student receives an interactive take‑home booklet designed to reinforce the lessons introduced during the play.

The booklets help children:

  • Practice the COLOR Method through guided activities
  • Reflect on emotions they experience in daily life
  • Express feelings through drawing and simple writing
  • Identify trusted adults they can turn to
  • Apply emotional skills both at school and at home

This ensures the play is not a one‑time event, but a long‑lasting emotional learning experience.
